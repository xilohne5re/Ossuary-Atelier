01 The Origin Story

I almost walked past it. It was folded flat on a shelf between a pile of polo shirts and a denim jacket three sizes too large — the kind of shelf where things get overlooked. The red caught first, then I saw the zipper running the full length of the front, and then I turned it over and read the label: Michiko Koshino.

I unfolded it on the spot. The upper half is a dense leopard jacquard — red ground, black marks, slightly bouclé in texture — and it cuts clean at the waist into a tight ribbed black lower half. The zip hardware is original, silver, still runs smooth. This piece was designed to be noticed. Someone stored it well and then let it go, which is the only reason it ended up on that shelf.

Michiko Koshino was designing in London from the late eighties onward — Japanese precision colliding with British club culture. This dress has that exact energy. The construction is confident, the print is not subtle, and whoever wore this first knew exactly what they were doing.

03 Care Instructions
◈
Hand wash cold, inside out — the jacquard upper is wool-blend and the print will fade and distort in a machine. Cold water, gentle detergent, no wringing.
◈
Reshape while damp, lay flat to dry — the knit will stretch out of shape if hung wet. Lay it flat on a towel in the original silhouette and leave it. Do not tumble dry under any circumstances.
◈
Steam the upper half only, never iron — the jacquard bouclé texture will crush flat under direct heat. A steamer held 4–5cm from the fabric is enough to release any creasing.
◈
Zip it up for storage — store with the zip closed to prevent the teeth from snagging the knit over time. Hang on a wide hanger or fold flat. Cedar block nearby if storing long-term.
