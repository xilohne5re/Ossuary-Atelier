01 The Origin Story

I was done. Four hours into Chatuchak on a Sunday in November, the kind of heat that makes the air taste like dust and fried garlic, and I had nothing. I had walked Section 8 twice, Section 2 three times, bought a bottle of water I didn't want just to stand in the shade of a vendor's awning for two minutes. I was already planning the route back to the exit.

The stall was in the covered passage between Sections 5 and 6 — the narrow one with low ceilings where the light goes yellow and everything smells like old fabric and incense. A woman was unloading a canvas bag onto a folding table, not a proper rack, just laying things out flat while she talked on the phone. I walked past. Then I stopped. Something in my peripheral vision registered wrong — too much colour, too dense, not the usual faded print. I turned around and went back.

It was face-down on the table. I turned it over. A hundred skulls looked back at me in yellow, red, blue, green, purple — stacked on top of each other, edge to edge, no background visible. I checked the label: GLAD NEWS. The zip ran from hem to collar and still moved clean. The woman hadn't finished unpacking. I paid before she hung it up. I don't think it was on sale for four minutes.

Glad News has been producing out of the Harajuku scene since the late nineties — gothic-punk graphics, skulls, studs, all-over prints — the brand that dressed the Harajuku kids that western fashion took another decade to catch up with. This all-over skull print sits in their early-2000s catalogue, when the graphics were densest and most unapologetic. Pieces from this era trade on Japanese secondhand markets at ¥8,000–¥30,000+. This one ended up on a folding table in Bangkok between Sections 5 and 6 of Chatuchak. I was almost at the exit.

03 Care Instructions
◈
Hand wash cold, zip closed — the all-over sublimation print covers every panel including the hood. Machine washing will crack and fade the ink unevenly over time. Cold water, a small amount of gentle detergent, no soaking beyond ten minutes.
◈
Wash inside out every single time — the print is the entire garment. Turning it inside out for every wash is the single most important thing you can do to preserve it over years of wear. Non-negotiable.
◈
Lay flat to dry, never tumble dry — the fleece base will shrink slightly under heat, and the zipper pull can snag the lining if tumbled. Lay flat, reshape hood by hand while damp, leave it.
◈
Steam from the inside only — if the fleece needs freshening, steam from the inside face only. Direct heat on the print surface will cause the ink to transfer and gloss. Never iron this piece.
