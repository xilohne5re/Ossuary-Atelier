DM Scripts

Pre-written messages for common situations. Keep this page bookmarked. When a moment arrives, copy, adjust the bracketed parts, and send — don't improvise under pressure.

Script 01 · Initial Pitch to Creator
Hey [name] — I run a small thrift brand called Ossuary Atelier, based in Thailand. We source and document rare vintage finds — each Archive piece has a QR tag that links to the full story of where it was found and what it is.

I've been following your page for a while and I think your aesthetic fits something I've got in mind. I'd love to offer you [ITEM-00X — brief description, e.g. "a 1990s Glad News skull hoodie vest"] as a collaboration piece — gifted, no cost to you.

In return I'd ask for 2 posts within 4 weeks tagging @ossuaryatelier, at least one being a Reel. Full details are in our partnership page: [link to partnership.html].

Let me know within the next 3 days if you're interested — happy to answer any questions before you decide. No pressure either way.
Keep it under 150 words. The item does the selling — your pitch just has to not get in the way. If they don't reply within 3 days, one follow-up is fine. After that, move on.
Script 02 · Return or Complaint Request (Casual Buyer)
Hey — thanks for reaching out. All our pieces are sold as final sale vintage, and condition is described in detail on each listing and story page.

If there's something that's significantly different from how we described it, I want to know — send me a photo of the issue and I'll take a look. If it's something we missed in our description, let's talk about how to make it right.

If it's a fit or style thing — totally understand, but that's on the measurements and description we provided, so returns aren't something we're able to do.
This holds the line without being aggressive. The phrase "let's talk about how to make it right" keeps the door open for genuine issues without promising anything. Only escalate to a partial refund if the flaw is clearly undisclosed and significant.
Script 03 · Confirming a Sale
Amazing — [item name] is yours. I'll hold it for 48 hours for payment.

Payment: [PromptPay / bank transfer details]
Total: ฿[price] + ฿[shipping if applicable]

Once payment is confirmed I'll package it up and dispatch within 3–5 business days. I'll send you the tracking number when it's on its way.

Please confirm your shipping address and let me know if you have any questions before I send payment details. Looking forward to it.
Clean, professional, no emoji overload. Sets the 48-hour window without making it feel like a threat. Asking for the address upfront avoids the most common cause of shipping delays.
Script 04 · Creator Late on Content
Hey [name] — just checking in. The 4-week window from our agreement is coming up [or: has just passed] and I haven't seen any posts yet.

No stress if something came up — just let me know where things are at. If you need a bit more time that's fine, just want to stay in touch.
One follow-up only. If they don't reply or engage after this, note it and don't offer them another piece. No confrontation necessary.
